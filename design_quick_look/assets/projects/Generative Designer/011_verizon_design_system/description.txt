Comprehensive design system translating Pentagram's Swiss design-inspired branding into cohesive, code-based interface components, improving consistency, efficiency, and accessibility across Verizon's digital products.

Challenge
• Build Verizon design system from ground up
• No central design organization or unified design system existed
• Designers distributed across engineering teams throughout organization
• Conceptual designs from agencies not easily translatable to code and lacked componentization

Audience
• Verizon's journey teams working on feature projects
• End users: Verizon's diverse customer base
• One of top utility communications companies in US
• Ensuring AAA accessibility crucial to serve all users effectively

Context
• Conducting audit on scale of Verizon required months of work
• Collaboration with two design agencies
• Agencies developed conceptual designs not easily translatable to code
• Designs lacked componentization
• Collaborated with Nathan Curtis from EightShapes to implement Brad Frost's Atomic Design Framework

Solution
• Created design system translating Pentagram's Swiss design-inspired branding into cohesive interface
• All components designed to be buildable and code-based
• Storybook implementations to ensure consistency and ease of use
• Comprehensive guidelines and templates
• Code-based components for seamless integration
• Scalable system that could be extended as needed

Results
• Worked closely with engineering teams to implement templates and components
• Collaborated with journey teams to provide examples and guidance
• Resulted in unified design approach
• Improved consistency, efficiency, and accessibility across Verizon's digital products

Year
• 2017 - 2019
Collaborators
• Brandy Bora, Nathan Curtis (EightShapes), Chunhung Wang, Ori Statlender
